# homestead.plus — Growth & Early Adoption Strategy

## The Core Challenge

homestead.plus is a marketplace — it has a cold-start problem on both sides. Customers won't come without a catalog; designers won't list without customers. The early strategy needs to solve the supply side first, because without a compelling catalog there's nothing to market.

---

## Early Adopter Target: Independent Designers

The best early-stage designers to target are those who are already selling on ABHP or similar platforms but have reasons to want more:

- **Identity** — their work is sold anonymously under ABHP's brand; they get no profile, no reputation, no direct relationship with buyers
- **Delivery frustration** — no standard delivery tool; they're patching together email, Dropbox, and whatever else
- **Revenue ceiling** — ABHP's cut and lack of upsell tools limits what they can earn per customer
- **Licensing gaps** — no pre-purchase license signing means enforcement is practically impossible

homestead.plus offers them a public profile, a real storefront, standardized delivery, and proper licensing tooling. That's a meaningful pitch.

---

## Business Spotlights

A "Designer Spotlight" (and eventually spotlights for other business types) is a high-leverage early-adopter acquisition tool:

- A dedicated blog post or specialized content piece highlighting a specific designer — their background, their design philosophy, featured plans
- The spotlight is published on homestead.plus's blog/editorial section before or at launch
- The designer gets genuine promotional value: a piece of content they can share, a public profile with backlinks, visibility to an audience they don't otherwise reach
- homestead.plus gets: early catalog content, a relationship with a real designer, and SEO-valuable content about real professionals in the space

This is a zero-cost acquisition loop if the content quality is high. Designers trade catalog access and early participation for promotion. homestead.plus trades content effort for supply-side growth.

### Spotlight Format (Draft)

- Designer intro: who they are, how long they've been designing, what draws them to residential floor plan design
- Philosophy: what makes a great floor plan, what they think about when designing for a specific style or family
- Featured plans: 2-3 of their listings on homestead.plus with a brief note on each
- Where to find them: link to their public profile on homestead.plus

Spotlights serve double duty as marketing content: they demonstrate that homestead.plus values and surfaces its designers (the anti-ABHP positioning), and they're the kind of human content that performs well in search and social.

---

## Public Business Profiles as a Differentiator

Public business profiles are already part of the platform design. The strategic point worth making explicit:

**This is the Amazon model, not the ABHP model.**

On ABHP, a customer has no idea who designed the floor plan they're buying or who will actually fulfill their order. On homestead.plus, the designer's name, profile, portfolio, and reputation are front and center — before, during, and after the purchase. This isn't just a UX choice; it's the philosophical foundation of the platform.

Implications:
- Product detail pages should prominently credit and link to the designer profile
- Order confirmations should name the designer and set expectations ("Your order will be fulfilled by [Designer Name]")
- Reviews (when implemented) are attached to the designer's profile, not just the product
- The designer's public profile should be linkable and shareable — something a designer would actually want to show off

---

## Competing While at ABHP

Steven currently works at ABHP. This shapes the launch timeline and approach:

- Spotlights and early designer relationships can be built before full launch — content doesn't require a live marketplace
- The platform can go live with a small curated catalog rather than waiting for scale
- Positioning should be honest and differentiated, not adversarial — the pitch to designers is about what homestead.plus offers them, not about ABHP being bad
- Nothing in the launch should create a legal or ethical conflict with Steven's employment; the platform should stand on its own merits

## Notes

- Spotlight content is also high-value for SEO — queries like "residential floor plan designer [name]" or "[style] house plan designers" are low competition and high intent
- Consider a "Featured Designer" rotation on the homepage once catalog grows
- Spotlight format could expand to other business types: Builder spotlights, Architect spotlights — each one reinforces the platform's professional network positioning
