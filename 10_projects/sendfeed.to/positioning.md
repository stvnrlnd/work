# sendfeed.to — Marketing Positioning & Messaging

## The Core Idea

sendfeed.to is the email digest tool for people who already have an audience and already have content — they just don't want to think about the plumbing.

The defining bet: users bring their own email credentials. sendfeed.to does the scheduling, feed polling, and digest assembly. It never touches your sending reputation, never locks your subscribers into its platform, and never asks you to publish through it.

---

## Positioning Statement

**For** independent publishers, bloggers, and content creators who want to send regular email digests to their subscribers, **sendfeed.to** is a bring-your-own-provider digest automation tool that reads your RSS feeds and delivers them on your schedule — using your own email credentials. **Unlike** newsletter platforms like Substack or Buttondown, sendfeed.to doesn't require you to publish through it, host your content on it, or rely on its sending infrastructure.

---

## Who It's For

**Primary — The Independent Blogger**
Runs a site on WordPress, Ghost, Statamic, or any platform that produces an RSS feed. Wants subscribers to get a digest on a cadence (weekly, monthly) without manually writing and sending a newsletter. Doesn't want to migrate content to a new platform to do it.

**Secondary — The Curator**
Pulls together multiple RSS feeds (their own or others') into a single digest for an audience. Think a niche newsletter that aggregates the best from a handful of sources.

**Not (yet) for:**
- High-volume senders who need advanced segmentation or analytics
- Teams (no multi-user/org support in MVP)
- Marketers running campaigns (this is for digests, not blasts)

---

## Key Differentiators

**1. Bring Your Own Provider**
You configure SMTP, Mailgun, Postmark, or Resend credentials once per project. Digests send from your account, your domain, your sending reputation. sendfeed.to is an orchestrator, not a provider. Inspired by Laravel Forge's model.

**2. Content lives where it already lives**
You don't publish through sendfeed.to. Your blog, your CMS, your platform — the RSS feed is the interface. No migration, no lock-in.

**3. Subscriber portability**
Subscribers are yours. You can export them at any time. They didn't sign up with sendfeed.to, they signed up with you.

**4. Simple pricing, predictable costs**
Flat monthly tiers based on feed count and subscriber volume. Add-on subscriber blocks at $1/1,000. No per-send fees, no surprise charges.

## Messaging by Audience

### Homepage headline options
- "Your RSS feeds. Your email provider. Your subscribers. Just automated."
- "Send email digests from your own feeds — using credentials you already own."
- "Set it once. Your content reaches your subscribers on schedule."

### Sub-headline
"Connect your RSS feed, add your SMTP credentials, set a schedule. sendfeed.to handles the rest — no platform lock-in, no shared sending infrastructure."

### Feature callouts
- **Any RSS feed** — Point to your blog, your podcast, your changelog, or any feed you want to aggregate
- **Your email provider** — Use Mailgun, Postmark, Resend, or plain SMTP. Your reputation, your deliverability
- **Flexible schedules** — Daily, weekly, or monthly. Sends at the time you choose
- **Subscriber management** — Add subscribers manually or import a CSV. Export any time

## Tone

- Direct and plain. No marketing fluff.
- Slightly technical — the target user is a developer or a developer-adjacent creator. They'll appreciate knowing how it works.
- Confident but not braggy. The product does one thing well; describe it simply.
- Avoid words like "powerful," "seamless," "robust," "effortless."

## Competitive Landscape (Brief)

| Tool | What they do | Why sendfeed.to is different |
|---|---|---|
| Substack | Newsletter platform + publishing + payments | Requires publishing on their platform; owns the subscriber relationship |
| Buttondown | Email newsletter tool | You write the newsletter manually; BYOP is not a feature |
| Mailchimp | Full email marketing suite | Complex, expensive, not RSS-native |
| Kill the Newsletter | Converts RSS to email for a single recipient | Read-only, personal use — no subscriber management or scheduling |
| FeedBlitz / Feedburner | RSS-to-email automation | Older tools; rely on their own sending infrastructure |

The gap sendfeed.to fills: RSS-native digest automation, bring-your-own email provider, clean subscriber management, modern pricing. Nobody owns this clearly.
