# openfaith.world — Overview

## What It Is

openfaith.world is a community hub that rises above specific religion to unite people in kindness, love, compassion, and understanding. It is not against religion, but it is not specifically for any religion either. The goal is a welcoming space where anyone — regardless of their beliefs — can feel like they belong.

The driving motivation: fatigue with religion that makes someone the bad guy to prove a point, or that unites people in hate rather than love. This is meant to be the alternative to that.

## Tech Stack

- **CMS:** Statamic (statically generated)
- **Hosting:** Cloudflare Pages
- **Publishing automation:** GitHub Action runs nightly to pull updated project files and publish any scheduled posts

## Target Audience

Anyone looking for community built around shared values — kindness, love, compassion, understanding — without being required to hold or abandon any particular religious belief.

## Monetization

Still being figured out. The core tension: the project is meant to have no barriers to belonging, so asking for money feels at odds with that.

### Current thinking

- **Single newsletter** with both free and premium content in every issue. Premium content is sermon-style — evergreen, spiritually resonant pieces. Recipients who pay get the full experience; the free content is still meaningful on its own.
- **Pay-what-you-want** model for the premium newsletter tier. No hard paywall — people pay what they can, including nothing.
- **Sponsors** — organizations or individuals who want to support the mission can donate. In return, thinking: premium newsletter content, a discount on content packs, and/or a profile with a sponsor badge on the site.
- **Content packs** — the primary fixed-price product. Sold to community leaders (or anyone), not pay-what-you-want. See below.

This is not meant to be a charity, but revenue should feel like an expression of support rather than a fee for access.

### Content Packs

An annual product, launched near the end of each year for the coming year. Each pack contains:

- **52–104 individual sermon-style pieces** (buyer's choice — weekly or twice-weekly, to match their community's meeting cadence)
- **Bonus evergreen materials** — bookmarks, brochures, and similar print/digital extras

Content packs are the only fixed-price product. Old packs (prior years) become the source material for the premium newsletter tier — so someone who couldn't afford the full pack at the start of the year can still receive that content over time via newsletter. This creates a natural content reuse pipeline: pack → newsletter.

The name "sermon" is a working label. The content fills that role but the right word for this context hasn't been landed on yet.

## Status

Live in production. Newsletter and monetization not yet active — blocked on [[sendfeed.to]] being production-ready, as that's the intended newsletter platform.
